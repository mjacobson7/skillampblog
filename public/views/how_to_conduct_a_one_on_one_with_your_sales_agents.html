The #1 Thing to Learn to be Successful in Sales

“If there is any one secret of success, it lies in the ability to get the other person’s point of view and see things from that person’s angle as well as from your own.”

Posted by Ryan Gray on March 19, 2018

The most effective way to ensure continued growth for your sales agents is regular one on one meetings. It is important that these one on one meetings happen every week. If you miss a one on one with your sales agent or cancel, you are sending a signal to that manager that they are not important. Have you ever been stood up on a date? It feels like that. Your sales agent is left alone at a restaurant while everyone else around has dates.

Disclaimer

Before I go over the 4 steps to conducting a successful one on one, there are a couple of things to keep in mind:

  1. Who Conducts The Meeting? Despite what a lot of supervisors or agents believe, the sales agent is in charge of conducting the one on one, not the supervisor. The sales agent will set the agenda before the meeting and is responsible for what will be discussed. The supervisor, in turn, is there to help and guide the sales agent to ensure goals are being met and objectives are being accomplished.

  2. What Are The Goals? It is important that every sales agent has clear goals that he or she needs to accomplish. These can be weekly, monthly and/or quarterly goals. This will depend on your company. Remember that goals are specific, measurable, and time-bound. Do your sales agents know what their goals are? Do you give them the necessary autonomy to accomplish them? This is important when setting expectations for the one on one.

  3. What Should Be Prepared Before The Meeting? Supervisors should audit calls from the sales agent and have specific advice on how he or she could improve. Like the goals, this council should be specific and measurable. The supervisor should also write down specific wins the sales agent had that week. On the other hand, sales agents should ask themselves the following questions: Am I still on track to accomplishing my goals? What obstacles are preventing me from succeeding? How can my supervisor help me accomplish my goals?

Conducting One on Ones

In any one on one meeting, there are 4 easy steps that must be followed every time. This ensures consistency across all your sales agents and continued growth for your team.


  • Step 1 - Goals: The sales agent discusses his or her goals and how they are doing. As a supervisor, you should listen. Do not interject. Let the sales agent discuss his or her problems and the potential solutions. Resist the urge to say anything here. Be a good listener.

  • Step 2 - Questions: Treat your sales agent like a future supervisor or director. Ask him or her questions about the potential problems or obstacles stopping the agent from succeeding. Let them try and solve the problem themselves. Here are some examples of questions you can ask: Why do you think you have not accomplished your goals? What could you do to better accomplish your goals? Now that you have achieved your goals, what should the next goal be? What are the top 3 things distracting you from your goals at work? etc...

  • Step 3 - Offer Help: This step is important but will only work if you are genuine. Show your agents you care and want them to succeed. Ask specifically how you can help. Offer suggestions or recommendations on what your agent should do next. Again, be genuine and patient. Your agents will not be forthright if they know other agents are chastised for being honest or seek help. Reward agents who ask for help or admit faults. Do not punish them.

  • Step 4 - Self- Reflection: The last question is extremely important. Ask your agent what you could do better to help them in achieving their goals. Ask ways that you could improve and better serve their needs. This may take a few tries to get genuine responses but, once you build trust with the agent, they will be more open. Once you get answers here, then ask how the company could be better at achieving its goals and how the company can better help its agents achieve theirs. The suggestions you get could really improve the company. Listen.

The Email After The Meeting

Once the one on one is over, be sure to write everything down. Have very specific action items for your sales agent and yourself. Take the agent’s suggestions seriously. Once you have it all written down, email it to your agent so he or she is aware of what you discussed. Give them an opportunity to edit your notes if you had something wrong. Once you both agree on the notes, be sure to bring them up next week with updates. You can also follow up throughout the week with your agents in person or through email. Getting your agents to trust you depends a lot on your ability to stay on top of these tasks.

And that is it. Rinse and repeat. In no time, you will have a goal-oriented call floor that is self-motivated to accomplish its goals.
